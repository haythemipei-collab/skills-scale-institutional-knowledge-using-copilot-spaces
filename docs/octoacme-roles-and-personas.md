# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Portfolio Owner

### Role Summary
Portfolio Owners oversee multiple projects and initiatives across the organization, ensuring alignment with strategic objectives. They facilitate prioritization, resolve resource conflicts, and maintain visibility across the project portfolio.

### Responsibilities
- Ensure projects align with organizational strategy and priorities
- Monitor cross-project dependencies and resource allocation
- Facilitate portfolio-level prioritization and trade-off decisions
- Escalate and resolve strategic risks that impact multiple projects
- Review and approve project charters and business cases
- Provide oversight on portfolio health metrics and reporting

### Goals
- Maximize strategic value across the entire portfolio
- Optimize resource utilization across projects
- Maintain portfolio-level visibility and governance
- Balance short-term delivery with long-term strategic goals

### Typical Communication
- Monthly portfolio reviews with executive leadership
- Quarterly strategic planning sessions
- Regular sync meetings with Project Sponsors and Project Managers
- Portfolio status reports and dashboards

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **With Project Sponsors**: Collaborates on project prioritization, resource allocation, and strategic alignment decisions
- **With Project Managers**: Reviews project health, dependencies, and resource needs; provides guidance on portfolio-level issues
- **With Product Managers**: Ensures product roadmaps align with portfolio strategy and organizational priorities
- **Escalation Path**: Receives escalations from Project Sponsors on strategic risks, resource conflicts, or cross-project dependencies

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors champion the project at the executive level, providing business context, resources, and strategic direction. They bridge the gap between the project team and senior leadership, ensuring the project delivers business value.

### Responsibilities
- Champion the project and secure necessary resources and budget
- Clarify business drivers, success criteria, and expected ROI
- Provide executive support and remove organizational obstacles
- Make key strategic decisions and approve major scope changes
- Review project progress and provide feedback to the Project Manager
- Communicate project value and outcomes to leadership

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers expected business outcomes and ROI
- Maintain executive alignment and support throughout delivery
- Remove organizational barriers that impede progress
- Validate that project remains aligned with business strategy

### Typical Communication
- Monthly steering committee meetings
- Executive status briefings and decision gates
- Project kickoff and milestone reviews
- Escalation point for critical issues requiring executive action

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **With Portfolio Owner**: Reports on project status, escalates resource conflicts and strategic risks
- **With Project Manager**: Receives regular status updates, provides strategic guidance, approves major changes and decisions
- **With Product Managers**: Validates product vision and business value, provides market and business context
- **With Stakeholders**: Ensures stakeholder alignment at executive level, facilitates key decisions
- **Handoff Points**: Engaged at project initiation (approval), major milestones (review), scope changes (approval), and project closure (sign-off)

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers drive adoption of process changes, scope modifications, or organizational transitions. They communicate impacts, coordinate training and documentation, and support teams through change transitions.

### Responsibilities
- Assess change impact on teams, processes, and stakeholders
- Develop and execute change management and communication plans
- Coordinate training, documentation updates, and support materials
- Monitor adoption metrics and identify resistance or risks
- Support stakeholders through transition periods
- Track and report on change adoption progress

### Goals
- Ensure smooth adoption of changes with minimal disruption
- Maintain stakeholder engagement and buy-in throughout transitions
- Reduce resistance and address concerns proactively
- Achieve target adoption rates within planned timelines

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments and readiness reports
- Training materials and knowledge transfer sessions
- Regular check-ins with affected teams and stakeholders
- Adoption metrics and feedback reports

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **With Project Manager**: Coordinates on timing of changes, identifies impacted stakeholders, integrates change activities into project plan
- **With Product Managers**: Aligns on feature rollout strategy and user communication
- **With Developers**: Works on technical documentation and training materials for new features or processes
- **With Stakeholders**: Communicates change impacts, gathers feedback, addresses concerns and resistance
- **Handoff Points**: Engaged during planning phase (impact assessment), pre-deployment (training and communication), and post-deployment (adoption monitoring)

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide specialized domain knowledge and technical expertise at key project stages. They advise on requirements, validate solutions, and ensure technical or domain standards are met.

### Responsibilities
- Provide domain-specific or technical expertise when needed
- Advise on requirements definition and solution design
- Review and validate technical approaches and deliverables
- Support risk identification related to specialized areas
- Participate in requirements reviews and design sessions
- Mentor team members on domain or technical topics

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet domain standards and best practices
- Reduce technical and domain-related risks through expert guidance
- Transfer knowledge to the delivery team when appropriate
- Validate solution quality from a specialized perspective

### Typical Communication
- Consultation sessions during key decision points
- Requirements reviews and design critiques
- Ad-hoc technical guidance and Q&A
- Knowledge sharing and documentation reviews

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **With Project Manager**: Consulted at key milestones requiring specialized input; helps identify domain-specific risks
- **With Developers**: Provides technical guidance, reviews designs and code, mentors on best practices
- **With Product Managers**: Advises on technical feasibility and domain constraints during requirements definition
- **With Technical Lead**: Collaborates on technical architecture and design decisions
- **Engagement Points**: Requirements definition phase, technical design reviews, solution validation, and acceptance testing

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads guide technical architecture decisions, mentor development staff, and ensure engineering standards and quality. They bridge technical execution with project objectives and identify technical risks.

### Responsibilities
- Make key technical architecture and design decisions
- Define and enforce coding standards and best practices
- Mentor and support developers on technical challenges
- Conduct design reviews and technical risk assessments
- Identify and resolve technical blockers
- Ensure code quality, maintainability, and scalability
- Guide technical trade-offs and solution approaches

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, maintainable solutions
- Build technical capability within the development team
- Minimize technical debt and quality issues
- Ensure architectural consistency across the project

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture diagrams
- Code review feedback and standards documentation
- Technical standups and architecture discussions
- Risk assessments for technical decisions

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Mentors on technical challenges, reviews code and designs, provides technical direction
- **With Project Manager**: Communicates technical risks, estimates, and dependencies; helps with technical planning
- **With Product Managers**: Advises on technical feasibility and trade-offs during feature planning
- **With SMEs**: Collaborates on specialized technical decisions requiring domain expertise
- **With QA/Testing**: Defines testing strategy and quality standards
- **Key Touchpoints**: Project kickoff (architecture), sprint planning (technical feasibility), code reviews (quality), and technical retrospectives (improvements)
